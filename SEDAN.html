Sedan

A sedan, or saloon, is a passenger car with a three-box design, featuring separate compartments for the engine, passengers, and cargo. Typically, sedans have four doors and seat four to five people. They come in various sizes and types, including compact, mid-size, full-size, luxury, and sport sedans.

Key Points

Comfort and Space: Known for spacious interiors and smooth rides. Trunk Space: Provides ample storage in a separate trunk. Fuel Efficiency: Many models are designed to be economical. Modern Features: Equipped with advanced infotainment and safety systems, with some offering hybrid and electric options.

Variants

Compact: Smaller, fuel-efficient (e.g., Toyota Corolla). Mid-Size: Balanced comfort and performance (e.g., Honda Accord). Full-Size: Larger and more spacious (e.g., Chevrolet Impala). Luxury: High-end, with premium features (e.g., BMW 5 Series). Sport: Performance-focused (e.g., BMW M3). Sedans remain popular for their practicality and efficiency, despite competition from SUVs and crossovers.
